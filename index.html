PROJECT

Skin cancer photo app

My Project is an app that lets you take a photo of a mole or freckle using your smart phone camera and then uses a machine learning algorithm to say what % chance the spot has of being cancerous and then adds the data to the machine learning program. It would use data from people continuously checking to see if they have cancerous moles as well as working with hospitals to have a data source to start with.


The motivation behind this is having worried about spots I have seen on my own skin and wishing that I could figure it out straight away instead of trying to self-diagnose through google and then having to go to a gp only to be told that no its not cancer. Another motivation is that two in three Australians will be diagnosed with skin cancer by the age of 70 and around 1800 australians will die each year (https://www.aihw.gov.au/reports/cancer/skin-cancer-in-australia/summary) . It wouldn’t surprise me if those numbers increase into the future as a result of global warming as well.


The app Will have a sleek UI and look and function similar to a normal camera app so that people of all generations will be able to use it. It will be as simple as taking a picture of a mark or spot and then depending on the accuracy may ask for multiple photos from different angles and then it will display the result on the screen, and link you to your nearest gp. Ideally the app would be recommended by health professionals and then it can partner with specific skin cancer organizations in your area. As the app began to get used it would improve its rate of predicting skin cancer as it will continuously gather more and more data. The app would always err on the side of caution as a false positive in most cases is better than a false negative. The app would be available on android and IOS. It will use computer visions and machine learning to perform accurate cancer checks and would be more reliable than a human examining the mole / mark. Other features it will provide will be Mole and mark tracking to see if any of them have changed and Tips and tricks to preventing skin cancer.


The app will use JavaScript as the front end and Python as the backend. It will need to use Computer vision models as well as having a large source of data to begin with so it can make accurate predictions from the start and only improve over time. It will need large servers to store the information as it won’t be able to run directly from your phone and will need a connection to the internet.


The skills required will be a large amount of experience with python and JavaScript as well as a background in machine learning and computer science. The skills will be hard to develop but shouldn’t be to hard to find as you wouldn’t need a permanent employ and could use various consultants. Sourcing the starting data may be the hardest part and would require some time and conversations with medical companies.


If this project is successful then the original problem would be partly solved as it would provide reassurance and save skin cancer experts a lot of time. Ideally it would also reduce the amount of deaths each year caused by skin cancer as it will increase early detection. It may increase the amount of skin cancer that is found each year but that is a bonus as it means that more people are finding it earlier.
